Section 1:
HB 165 2026
1 A bill to be entitled
2 An act relating to law enforcement, correctional, and
3 institutional security specialist officers; providing
4 a short title; providing legislative findings and
5 intent; requiring the average salary of a correctional
6 officer, correctional probation officer, or
7 institutional security specialist to be no less than a
8 certain percentage of the average salaries of sworn
9 law enforcement officers; requiring the Department of
10 Management Services to submit an annual compensation
11 analysis report to the Governor, the President of the
12 Senate, and the Speaker of the House of
13 Representatives; requiring an annual salary increase
14 for sworn law enforcement officers, correctional
15 officers, correctional probation officers, and
16 institutional security specialists; amending s.
17 252.36, F.S.; providing that the Governor may
18 authorize certain correctional officers, correctional
19 probation officers, and institutional security
20 specialists to assist with law enforcement operations
21 during a declared state or federal emergency;
22 providing an effective date.
23
24 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Florida:
25

26 Section 1. This act may be cited as the "Correctional and
27 Probation Officer Fairness Act."
28 Section 2. Compensation of correctional officers,
29 correctional probation officers, and institutional security
30 specialists.—
31 (1) The Legislature finds that:
32 (a) Correctional officers, correctional probation
33 officers, and institutional security specialists are certified
34 by the Criminal Justice Standards and Training Commission and
35 are held to the same professional standards as state law
36 enforcement officers.
37 (b) Correctional officers, correctional probation
38 officers, and institutional security specialists work in high-
39 risk environments and serve a vital role in public safety,
40 security, and offender supervision.
41 (c) There are disparities in compensation among
42 correctional officers, correctional probation officers, and
43 institutional security specialists and state law enforcement
44 officers.
45 (2) It is the intent of the Legislature to:
46 (a) Ensure that correctional officers, correctional
47 probation officers, and institutional security specialists
48 receive compensation consistent with that of state law
49 enforcement officers.
50 (b) Provide that the Governor may authorize emergency

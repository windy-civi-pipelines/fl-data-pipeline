Section 1:
HB 4001 2026
1 A bill to be entitled
2 An act relating to Jefferson County; providing
3 applicability; providing definitions; limiting
4 compensation to a health care provider that provides
5 medical services for an inmate housed in a Jefferson
6 County detention center if the provider does not have
7 a contract with the county to provide such services;
8 limiting compensation to an entity that provides
9 emergency medical transportation services for an
10 inmate housed in a Jefferson County detention center
11 if the entity does not have a contract with the county
12 to provide such services; providing applicability;
13 providing an effective date.
14
15 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Florida:
16
17 Section 1. (1) Notwithstanding any other law to the
18 contrary, this act and all rules adopted pursuant to this act
19 apply to the area located within Jefferson County.
20 (2) As used in this act, the term:
21 (a) "Emergency medical transportation services" includes,
22 but is not limited to, services rendered by means of air
23 ambulance or an emergency medical services vehicle. As used in
24 this paragraph, the term:
25 1. "Air ambulance" means a fixed-wing or rotary-wing

26 aircraft used for, or intended to be used for, air
27 transportation of sick or injured persons requiring or likely to
28 require medical attention during transport.
29 2. "Emergency medical services vehicle" means a privately
30 or publicly owned vehicle or vessel that is designed,
31 constructed, reconstructed, maintained, equipped, or operated
32 for, and is used or intended to be used for, land or water
33 transportation of sick or injured persons requiring or likely to
34 require medical attention during transport.
35 (b) "Health care provider" means:
36 1. A hospital licensed under chapter 395, Florida
37 Statutes.
38 2. A physician or physician assistant licensed under
39 chapter 458, Florida Statutes.
40 3. An osteopathic physician or physician assistant
41 licensed under chapter 459, Florida Statutes.
42 4. A podiatric physician licensed under chapter 461,
43 Florida Statutes.
44 5. A health maintenance organization certificated under
45 part I of chapter 641, Florida Statutes.
46 6. An ambulatory surgical center licensed under chapter
47 395, Florida Statutes.
48 7. An other medical facility. As used in this
49 subparagraph, the term "other medical facility" means a facility
50 the primary purpose of which is to provide human medical

52 medical treatment, to which facility the patient is admitted and

59 8. A professional association, partnership, corporation,
60 joint venture, or other association composed of the persons

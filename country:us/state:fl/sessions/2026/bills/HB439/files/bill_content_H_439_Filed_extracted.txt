Section 1:
HB 439 2026
1 A bill to be entitled
2 An act relating to the practice of chiropractic
3 medicine; amending s. 460.403, F.S.; revising the
4 definition of the term "practice of chiropractic
5 medicine"; authorizing chiropractic physicians to
6 possess, prescribe, and administer vitamins, nutrient
7 preparations, homeopathic remedies, dietary
8 supplements, and epinephrine, by specified means;
9 requiring chiropractic physicians to be certified by
10 the Board of Chiropractic Medicine to administer by
11 injection vitamins, nutrient preparations, homeopathic
12 remedies, dietary supplements, and epinephrine;
13 authorizing licensed pharmacists to fill, compound, or
14 dispense certain prescriptions for licensed
15 chiropractic physicians under certain circumstances;
16 providing requirements for the certification program;
17 providing an effective date.
18
19 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Florida:
20
21 Section 1. Paragraph (c) of subsection (9) of section
22 460.403, Florida Statutes, is amended to read:
23 460.403 Definitions.—As used in this chapter, the term:
24 (9)
25 (c)1. Chiropractic physicians may adjust, manipulate, or

55 3. A chiropractic physician may only administer by

Section 1:
HB 73 2026
1 A bill to be entitled
2 An act relating to restoration of voting rights
3 database; amending s. 20.32, F.S.; requiring the
4 Florida Commission on Offender Review to develop and
5 maintain a database containing certain information for
6 a certain purpose; requiring certain governmental
7 entities to provide certain information to the
8 commission on a monthly basis; requiring the
9 Department of Management Services, acting through the
10 Florida Digital Service, to provide certain technical
11 assistance to the commission; authorizing the
12 department to adopt rules; requiring the commission to
13 make available to the public the database on an
14 Internet website by a date certain; requiring the
15 commission to update the database monthly and publish
16 certain information on the website; requiring the
17 commission to provide to the Governor and Legislature
18 a comprehensive plan by a date certain; providing
19 requirements for the comprehensive plan; providing
20 that a person who takes certain actions in reasonable
21 reliance on the database may not be charged with
22 certain violations of criminal law; requiring the
23 commission to adopt rules; providing an effective
24 date.

HB 73 2026
26 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Florida:
27
28 Section 1. Subsection (4) is added to section 20.32,
29 Florida Statutes, to read:
30 20.32 Florida Commission on Offender Review.—
31 (4)(a) For the purpose of assisting a person who has been
32 disqualified from voting based on a felony conviction other than
33 murder or a felony sexual offense in determining whether he or
34 she has met the requirements under s. 98.0751 and had his or her
35 voting rights restored pursuant to s. 4, Art. VI of the State
36 Constitution, the commission shall develop and maintain a
37 database that contains for each such person all of the following
38 information:
39 1. His or her name and any other personal identifying
40 information.
41 2. The remaining length of any term of supervision,
42 including, but not limited to, probation, community control, or
43 parole, ordered by a court as a part of his or her sentence.
44 3. The remaining amount of any restitution owed to a
45 victim as ordered by a court as a part of his or her sentence.
46 4. The remaining amount due of any fines or fees that were
47 initially ordered by a court as a part of his or her sentence or
48 as a condition of any form of supervision, including, but not
49 limited to, probation, community control, or parole.
50 5. The completion status of any other term ordered by a

52 6. Any other information needed to determine whether he or
53 she has met the requirements for restoration of voting rights
54 under s. 98.0751.
55 (b) Each month, the Department of State, the Department of
56 Corrections, the clerks of the circuit courts, the county
57 comptrollers, and the Board of Executive Clemency shall provide

71 murder or a felony sexual offense may follow to have his or her
72 voting rights restored and to register to vote.
